# Adaptive agent risk routing

Iteration 5 makes the protected agent runtime risk-aware without turning risk classification into another free-form agent opinion.

## Commands

```bash
selenium-pw-migrator migration assess-agent-risk --out migration/runs/wave-001
selenium-pw-migrator migration next-agent-action --out migration/runs/wave-001
selenium-pw-migrator migration check-agent-budget --out migration/runs/wave-001
selenium-pw-migrator migration agent-perf-report --out migration/runs/wave-001
```

`assess-agent-risk` writes `agent-risk-assessment.json`. The assessment is deterministic for the current wave evidence and contains:

- a score from 0 to 100;
- `low`, `medium`, `high`, or `critical` level;
- evidence-backed reasons and weights;
- an assessment fingerprint;
- recommended roles;
- the adaptive role budget;
- whether automatic continuation is allowed.

## Signals

The router evaluates only runtime-owned or deterministic evidence:

- wave planning risk and complexity-budget status;
- validation and wave-contract state;
- no-progress evidence;
- changed/deleted/protected paths;
- TODO and unmapped counts;
- review-bundle risk flags;
- repeated or failed role invocations.

It does not infer risk from prose generated by the role itself.

## Adaptive budgets

A low-risk `fast` run receives a maximum of four automatic role invocations. It may use up to two executor turns, one final reviewer, and one final sentinel; watchdog capacity is zero until deterministic evidence raises the risk.

Medium and high risk restore watchdog capacity and larger bounded budgets. `standard` and `audit` retain their stronger pre-execution obligations. Final reviewer, final sentinel, scope checks, and final gate are mandatory in every level.

## Critical risk

Blocking evidence such as gate/assertion weakening, evidence manipulation, invalid wave contracts, or hard complexity-budget violations produces `critical` risk and `HUMAN_REVIEW_REQUIRED`. The runtime does not silently promote itself to a more permissive profile.

A `RUN_ROLE` decision includes `riskAssessmentFingerprint`. Before accepting `STARTED`, the runtime recomputes the assessment. If the evidence changed, the dispatch is stale and the caller must run `next-agent-action` again.

## Performance evidence

`agent-lifecycle-performance.json` now includes risk score, risk level, assessment fingerprint, adaptive lifecycle budget, and `lifecycleBudgetStatus`. Wall-clock budget is diagnostic because human idle time may be present; role-count budgets remain the hard automatic-continuation guard.
